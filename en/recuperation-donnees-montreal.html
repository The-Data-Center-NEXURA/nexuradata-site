Data recovery in Montreal

This service covers cases where the primary goal is to regain access to lost, deleted or inaccessible data on a drive, SSD, server or external device.

When recovery becomes necessary

  • The drive is no longer detected or has become unstable.
  • Files were deleted or a volume was formatted.
  • An external device shows errors or abnormal behaviour.
  • A workstation or server contains critical data that is no longer accessible.

Common support types

  • Internal and external hard drives.
  • SATA and NVMe SSDs.
  • USB keys and memory cards.
  • Servers, NAS and business storage environments.

What needs to be clarified quickly

Which device is involved, whether the issue looks logical or physical, whether the media should be stopped immediately and how urgent the case really is.

When this service is the right fit

It fits situations where the priority is to recover files, restore access to a device or assess unstable media before the situation gets worse.

Frequently asked questions

Data recovery

My drive is no longer detected — what should I do?

Stop all read or write attempts immediately. Every additional access can overwrite recoverable data. Describe the symptoms in your first contact so the case can be properly assessed.

What is the difference between a logical and a physical failure?

A logical failure affects the file system (deletion, formatting, corruption). A physical failure affects mechanical or electronic components. Treatment differs and must be determined by a professional triage.

How long does data recovery take?

Simple cases (logical deletion) can be handled in 1 to 3 business days. Complex cases (physical failure, RAID, SSD) typically take 3 to 10 days. A timeline is confirmed in the quote.

Do I need to drop off my drive or can I ship it?

Both options are available. The media can be dropped off at the lab or shipped under terms confirmed in the quote. Details are established at the first contact.

Do you recover data from Mac and Windows systems?

Yes. HFS+, APFS, NTFS, exFAT, ext4 and other file systems are handled. Compatibility is assessed during triage.
