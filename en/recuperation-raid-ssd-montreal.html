Technical cases

RAID, SSD and server recovery

This service covers higher-risk cases where a rebuild, forced reboot or unnecessary write operation can materially reduce recovery options.

Typical scenarios

  • Degraded or failed RAID arrays.
  • NAS or server storage no longer mounting cleanly.
  • SSD failures with unstable detection or severe logical corruption.
  • Business storage where continuity and data integrity matter.

Why these cases need caution

RAID and SSD cases are often made worse by improvised rebuilds, repeated boots or software attempts that write back to unstable media.

What matters first

Device count, RAID level, prior attempts, urgency and whether the environment is still running all change the intervention path.

How pricing is handled

These cases are assessed and quoted individually because scope depends on topology, damage, urgency and the real intervention required.
