When this offer fits

  • Broken phone, unusable screen or device that no longer boots properly.

Why this path is different

Mobile devices do not follow the same logic as a classic desktop drive. Encryption, locking state, cloud sync behaviour and hardware fragility all change how the intervention needs to be qualified.

What to avoid

  • Continuing to use, update or resync the device if the data is critical.
  • Repeated unlock attempts, reconnects or reboots if the device is unstable or overheating.
  • Mixing recovery and evidence goals without clarifying the mandate context.

Frequently asked questions

Mobile recovery

My phone is broken — is the data still recoverable?

In many cases, yes. Even if the screen is unusable or the device no longer powers on, the data may still be physically accessible. A no-charge assessment determines the available options.

Do you recover data from iPhone and Android?

Yes. Both platforms are covered. Results depend on the model, OS version, type of damage and whether active encryption is present. These factors are assessed during Assessment.

Should I keep using my phone in the meantime?

No. Any further use — calls, sync, updates, photos — can overwrite recoverable data. Put the device in airplane mode and contact us promptly.

What can be recovered from a phone?

Photos, videos, messages, contacts, documents, notes and history depending on the model and condition of the device. Third-party apps have varying levels of recoverability.

Is phone recovery covered by the free assessment?

Yes. As with all media types, the initial assessment is free. No fees are incurred before you approve the written quote.
