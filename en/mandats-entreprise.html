Service-Level Commitments (SLA)

Enterprise engagements include documented service-level commitments:

  • Acknowledgment — within 4 business hours of initial contact.
  • Preliminary assessment — technical quote within 24 hours of receiving the media.
  • Progress reports — structured updates at each key milestone of the engagement.
  • Emergency — after-hours intake accepted 7 days a week.

Service-level commitments are formalized in a signed agreement before work begins. Terms are adjusted based on case complexity.

Preservation & Chain of Custody

Every piece of media entrusted to NEXURADATA is handled with the same level of rigor, whether it's headed to litigation or not. The protocol includes:

  • Intake log — identity of the mandating party, date/time, condition of media, photographs.
  • Controlled handling — systematic use of hardware write blockers, full forensic imaging before any analysis.
  • Full traceability — every transfer, copy, or access is documented with timestamps and handler identity.
  • Secure return — media returned with signed release form. Additional data encrypted or destroyed per instructions.

Incident Response

When an incident affects an organization's data — ransomware, critical failure, data theft, or suspected fraud — NEXURADATA responds with a structured methodology:

  • Assessment — scope identification, affected media assessment, recovery priority setting.
  • Preservation — forensic copy of all relevant media before any recovery attempt.
  • Analysis — extraction, reconstruction, and analysis aligned with engagement objectives.
  • Report — structured deliverable with timeline, technical findings, and recommendations, written for non-technical readers.
  • Court availability — if the case evolves toward litigation, the examiner can appear as an expert witness.

Frequently asked

What organizations ask

What is the difference between a standard and an enterprise engagement?

An enterprise engagement includes a signed SLA, structured progress reports, documented chain of custody, and deliverables tailored for decision-makers, legal counsel, and insurers. Technical rigor is identical across all engagements.

How does billing work for an enterprise engagement?

The initial assessment is always free. For enterprise mandates, pricing is established in a written quote before any work begins. Recurring or priority arrangements can be negotiated under a service agreement.

Can we review your credentials before committing?

Yes. A capabilities brief is available on request: CFE certification, court experience, E&O coverage, and Law 25 compliance protocols. No commitment is required to receive it.

Are your reports admissible before Canadian courts?

Yes. Reports are prepared to recognized forensic standards. The examiner holds CFE certification and can appear as an expert witness before federal and provincial courts in Canada.

How do we engage NEXURADATA for an urgent incident?

Describe the incident via the contact form or directly by email at contact@nexuradata.ca, specifying the criticality level. Emergencies are accepted 7 days a week.
