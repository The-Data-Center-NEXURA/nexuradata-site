Investigation · Certified Forensic Examiner (CFE)

Digital forensics in Montreal

CFE-certified examiner with testimony experience before federal and provincial courts. Documented chain of custody, structured reports for decision-makers and counsel, incident response with full media preservation.

Forensic methodology

  • Preservation — systematic hardware write blockers, full forensic imaging (dd/dcfldd, FTK Imager) before any analysis.
  • Acquisition — bit-for-bit copy verified by cryptographic hash (SHA-256), documented chain of custody from intake to return.
  • Analysis — timeline reconstruction, targeted extraction, artifact correlation using EnCase, Autopsy, proprietary tools, and custom scripts.
  • Report — structured deliverable designed for decision-makers, lawyers, and insurers — not just technicians.

Frequent scenarios

  • Internal incident, fraud, or suspicious activity on a workstation or account.
  • Need to preserve a device or account before review.
  • Ransomware, compromise, or suspected exfiltration.
  • Cases that require a clear chronology of events.
  • Digital evidence preparation for civil or criminal litigation.

Guarantees for mandating parties

  • CFE certification — internationally recognized qualification in digital forensic analysis.
  • Court experience — expert reports and testimony before federal and provincial courts.
  • E&O insurance — active professional liability coverage for every engagement.
  • Full technical arsenal — FTK Imager, EnCase, Autopsy, Cellebrite, hardware write blockers, mobile analysis tools, and custom scripts.
  • Law 25 compliance — minimal collection, encryption, controlled destruction after delivery.

Who this service is for

  • Law firms and outside experts.
  • Healthcare enterprises — medical records, clinical systems.
  • SMBs and enterprises facing a sensitive incident.
  • Insurers and risk managers.
  • Internal IT and cybersecurity teams.
